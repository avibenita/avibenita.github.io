Cluster map (K-means)
How to read the cluster map
  • Each dot is one case (row of your data); its colour is the cluster it was assigned to.
  • The larger semi-transparent circles mark each cluster's center.
  • PCA (auto) projects all variables onto the two directions that preserve the most variance — the best flat view of the full variable space. The percentages on the axes show how much variance each component captures.
  • Variables lets you plot any two original variables in their raw units instead.
  • Well-separated clusters appear as tight same-coloured groups with clearly apart centers. Heavy overlap suggests weak separation — cross-check the silhouette on the Diagnostics page.
  • The distance table below the map gives the exact center-to-center distances behind what you see here.
  • Center trails (PCA view) trace each center's walk from its random starting seed to its final position — one dashed line per cluster, ending at the big center marker. Short direct trails mean the solution settled quickly; long meandering ones hint at an unstable start (note the path itself depends on the random seed).
  • Drag to zoom into a region; click a legend entry to hide or show a cluster.

Convergence
Converged: iWhether assignments stopped changing before the iteration cap. A non-converged run may still be improving — increase the iteration limit or reduce k.…
Iterations: iNumber of assign-and-update rounds K-means needed. Few iterations with convergence indicates a stable solution.…
Within-cluster SS (WCSS): iThe objective K-means minimises: total squared distance of each case to its cluster center. Lower is tighter, but WCSS always drops as k grows — compare it across k rather than reading it in isolation.…
Variance decomposition
Between-cluster SS: iSpread of the cluster centers around the overall mean — the part of total variability the clustering captures. Total SS = between SS + within SS.…
Explained variance: iBetween SS as a share of total SS (like R² for clustering). ≥ 50% is usually a meaningful partition; near 0% means the clusters barely differ from random splits.…
Average silhouette: iMean silhouette over all cases, from −1 to +1: how much closer each case is to its own cluster than the nearest other cluster. ≥ 0.5 reasonable, ≥ 0.7 strong, < 0.25 little real structure.…
Convergence process iHow the run settled, iteration by iteration. The line is WCSS (the objective K-means minimises) after each update; the columns are how many cases switched cluster that round. A steep-then-flat line with columns hitting zero means a clean, stable solution; columns still non-zero at the last iteration mean the run hit its cap before converging.
The line tracks WCSS falling as centers settle; the columns count cases reassigned each iteration, reaching zero at convergence. A long tail of small reassignments suggests points oscillating on a weak boundary between two clusters.
