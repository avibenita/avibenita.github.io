Intraclass Correlation Coefficient (ICC)

What ICC tells you

The ICC quantifies the proportion of total variance attributable to between-group differences. ICC ≥ 0.10 justifies a multilevel approach; ICC ≥ 0.30 indicates substantial clustering.

Variance Explained (Pseudo R²)
Marginal R² (Fixed only)
R²m: Marginal R² — proportion of variance explained by fixed effects alone, ignoring group-level clustering. Formula: σ²_fixed / (σ²_fixed + σ²_random + σ²_residual). …
Variance (Fixed): Variance of the fitted values from the fixed-effects part of the model only (σ²_fixed). Used as the numerator of R²_m. …
Conditional R² (Fixed + Random)
R²c: Conditional R² — proportion of variance explained by both fixed and random effects together. R²_c − R²_m is the unique contribution of clustering. …
Variance (Residual): Residual (within-group) variance σ²_ε — unexplained variance after accounting for all fixed and random effects. …

Nakagawa & Schielzeth (2013)

R²m is the variance explained by fixed effects alone. R²c includes both fixed and random effects. The difference (R²c − R²m) is the variance explained by clustering.
